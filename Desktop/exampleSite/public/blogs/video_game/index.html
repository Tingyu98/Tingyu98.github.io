Global Video Game Market Analysis

View on GitHub

Key Highlights

  • Analyzed global video game market trends (1980–2020) using multi-source datasets
  • Integrated sales, ratings, and economic data (GDP) for comprehensive analysis
  • Identified key drivers of game success across genres, platforms, and regions
  • Built interactive Power BI dashboard for dynamic exploration of market trends

Overview

This project analyzes global video game market trends to understand the factors influencing sales performance across regions, genres, platforms, and publishers.

By combining exploratory data analysis, visualization, and economic correlation analysis, the project provides insights into consumer behavior and market dynamics, supporting strategic decision-making for developers and publishers.

Key Questions

  • What genres, platforms, and publishers drive the highest sales?
  • How do regional markets differ in performance and behavior?
  • What is the relationship between GDP and video game sales?
  • How do ratings and reviews influence commercial success?

Methodology

Data Sources:

  • Video game sales dataset (Kaggle)
  • ESRB ratings dataset
  • IGN scores dataset
  • Steam reviews dataset
  • GDP per capita (World Bank)

Data Processing:

  • Cleaned and merged multiple datasets
  • Standardized formats across sources
  • Performed feature alignment across sales, ratings, and economic indicators

Analysis:

  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
  • Trend analysis across time, region, and genre
  • Correlation analysis between GDP and sales
  • Consumer behavior analysis using ratings and reviews

Visualization:

  • R (ggplot2, dplyr, tidyverse, Plotly)
  • Power BI dashboard for interactive exploration

Results & Insights

Market Trends

  • Global sales peaked in the late 2000s (~678.9M units), followed by a decline due to market saturation and rise of mobile/online gaming
  • Action games became the dominant genre after 2010, surpassing sports
  • Leading platforms included PS2, Xbox 360, and PS3, with Wii strong in casual gaming
  • North America led total sales, followed by Europe and Japan
  • Nintendo dominated global sales, followed by EA and Activision

Economic Correlation

  • Video game sales aligned with GDP trends prior to the 2008 financial crisis
  • Post-crisis, GDP recovered while sales lagged in North America and Europe
  • Japan maintained stable sales despite GDP fluctuations, indicating market resilience

Consumer Behavior

  • Higher IGN ratings (>7.5) generally correlated with higher sales
  • Diminishing returns observed beyond high rating thresholds
  • Steam reviews showed variability, with some high-selling games receiving moderate ratings
